One to One Volunteers

Benefits of being a Volunteer

Our Literacy Volunteers help children in schools and early years settings. They do not teach reading. Instead they help children on a one-to-one basis to give them confidence in their reading and communication skills through conversation, reading, writing and playing games.

In addition to the training, travelling expenses and on-going support we will provide you with. You also get to enjoy a volunteeritng role that is transforming the lives of children. Half a day a week of your time to have fun and help to educate is making all the difference in a child's life.

Learn More
Volunteers Must:
  • Like children.
  • Enjoy reading and communicating.
  • Be patient, flexible and reliable.
Volunteers:
  • Go to the same setting regularly for one morning or afternoon session each week.
  • Help children on an individual basis.
  • Help the same children each week (on average you see 4 children in a session).
  • Work outside of the classroom.
  • Talk, read, play games/do puzzles and write with their children.
  • Enjoy their time with the children and make sure that the children enjoy the sessions too
  • Make a real difference to a child
  • Promote a love of reading and books
Volunteers are provided with:
  • Training.
  • On-going support from an Area Co-ordinator.
  • Books, games and other resources to use with their children.
  • Travelling expenses.

INTERESTED? Contact us for more details and to enquire about training on 0115 876 5474 or contact@literacyvolunteers.org.uk

What we ask for

All that we ask is that you are someone who loves working with children, enjoys reading and comminicating with them and are capable of being patient, flexible in your schedule and dedicated to our mission of helping children all across Nottingham.

In addition to this, we invite you to attend out training sessions and learn a little more about what it means to be a part of our volunteer group, and how we go about teaching in schools.

Training

If you are interested in becoming a Literacy Volunteer in either an early years setting or primary, come along to one of our Training Courses and learn all about our organisation and how you can give your time to help children to love books and improve their literacy and communication skills.

We need Volunteers throughout Nottinghamshire, Derbyshire and some of Leicestershire so please call us to find out more or to book a place on one of the training courses. NOTE: YOU ARE REQUIRED TO ATTEND BOTH SESSION 1 AND 2 OF THE COURSE OFFERED TO YOU.

VETTING: You will also need to complete a Disclosure and Barring Service application and an online safeguarding course and online prevent course as part of your vetting.

Story Sharers

About Story Sharers

Story Sharers will make a key contribution to local children’s literacy, communication and comprehension skills. You will read to and with children to enthuse and raise the amount of time they spend ‘reading for pleasure’. We know that children who enjoy reading for pleasure are four time more likely to read at the expected level for their age than children who do not enjoy reading. You will act as a positive ambassador for reading, encouraging children to see books as offering them valuable and enjoyable experiences. You will increase children’s confidence and self-esteem. We’re not looking for professional storytellers – just people who are enthusiastic about, stories, books and reading and poetry.

Learn More
How it works

Literacy Volunteers will give you full training and regular support once you are matched with a setting. You will be provided with either story sack or story bag of resources to use (depending on the age of the children you are reading with and to).

Where? Early years / preschool settings, school breakfast clubs, during school lunchtimes and at after school provision. We can tailor the setting to suit you.

Who with? From early years (up to age 5), up to Year 6 (age 10/11).

When? This opportunity has been developed for volunteers who can only attend settings for a minimum of one hour a week for as little as one term.

We need you to:
  • Like children.
  • Enjoy reading and communicating.
  • Be patient, flexible and reliable.
  • Use a range of resources in an inspiring, fun and considered way.
  • Be flexible and independent in a modern educational setting.
  • Commit to safeguarding the welfare of the children.
You will:
  • Be valued as part of a team making a real difference to young children's future prospects.
  • Develop and use your skills over a period of time.
  • Receive induction and safeguarding training.
  • Work outside of the classroom.
  • Have on-going support from an Area Co-ordinator.
  • Be provided with resources.
  • Receive travel expenses.
