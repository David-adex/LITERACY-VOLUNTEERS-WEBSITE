Covid Measures Update

As we started the New Year and a new term, we had the new variant to cope with. Thankfully many of the Volunteers who had been in last term have been happy to go back into school. A few Volunteers who are clinically vulnerable are delaying their return but are hoping to be back in school very soon.

In school, Volunteers are carrying out lateral flow tests before going to school. They are wearing face coverings in school and schools have kept up the cleaning and hygiene procedures. All the procedures are agreed with the Volunteer and the school before a Volunteer starts or returns. Volunteers are happy to be back and the children are loving their sessions.

If you have any questions or concerns please get in touch with your Area Co- ordinator who will get back to you as soon as they can to help and support you.

The latest information regarding isolating after a positive test in England (17/09/2022 https://www.gov.uk/coronavirus) is as follows: You can stop self-isolating at the start of day 6 if you get 2 negative rapid lateral flow test results on days 5 and 6 and do not have a temperature. Tests must be at least 24 hours apart. If either test is positive, wait 24 hours before testing again.

Volunteer Profile—Barry Parker

Firstly, just a little about me, for no other reason than it has a bearing on my motivation to become a Literacy Volunteer.

After eight years in the Armed Forces (Royal Marines) I spent most of the rest of my working life with BT, where I was lucky enough to complete, an OU BSc. based primarily in Psychology. When I was in my early teens, although I was a competent reader I did not read regularly, and it was only the encouragement of an older sister that converted me to becoming a lifelong reading advocate. I firmly believe that the gift of reading is the key to self-fulfilment, not just the acquisition of knowledge, but also its ability to free you from the here and now.

So, after retiring some years ago and casting around worthwhile projects I finally picked up some information about Literacy Volunteers and after training at a very difficult time through Covid I attended my first session at Mansfield Primary Academy, and to say it was inspirational is an understatement.

The school were clear that the children should not use Volunteers first names, so I asked to be called Mister Barry, in an attempt make it more friendly. After an introduction by the teacher to the class, to hear the whole of year one shout hello Mr Barry was heart-warming in the extreme. I have, more recently, reached agreement with each child that I see, to not say “I can’t read that” but to say, “I can’t read that yet”, which always seems to see me rewarded with a smile. They all appear to enjoy our sessions together and I have seen indications of improvement especially with “blending” and general confidence.

I do realise that I need to constantly consider better ways to reach these children, especially those who perhaps, do not get the support from home. For example, I’m considering asking them to tell me “why is it a good thing to be able to read well”

Early Years Sessions Update

Our Learning to Love Books project moved from Zoom back to face to face sessions in October. It was wonderful to see the families in person finally and meet some new ones too. There are two sessions running weekly in Hyson Green and St Ann’s and we are sharing stories, ideas for parents/carers to help support their child’s early literacy and reading development and craft, songs and fun. We ran an outdoor storytelling session in St Ann’s Community Orchard which was great fun and had really good feedback from the families. We’re hoping to do another one in the Spring.
